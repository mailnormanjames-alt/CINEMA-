Approach

I build the machine that makes films possible.

My grandfather ran a textile factory in Lyon's Croix-Rousse. I grew up watching him manage 200 workers, coordinate deliveries across three time zones, and still make it home for dinner. Production management is in my blood — I just apply it to celluloid instead of silk.

After ten years as a line producer on other people's films, I started Renard Production to develop my own projects. The first commission I ever got was a documentary about Lyon's last working Jacquard loom. The director needed access to a factory that hadn't allowed cameras in forty years. I made three phone calls and we were shooting the next week.

Twelve films later, I still get the same satisfaction from solving the impossible logistics that my grandfather found in getting a silk order to Tokyo by Thursday.

"The best compliment I can receive is when a director says they forgot about the budget entirely. That means I did my job."
