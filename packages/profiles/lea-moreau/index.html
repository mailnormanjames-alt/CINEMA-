Act I — The Voice

I don't write dialogue.

I write the breath

between what characters

can no longer say.

— Léa Moreau, in her notebook

Every script is a negotiation between the visible and the hidden. The best scenes are the ones where the audience leans forward, unsure if they're watching or listening.

Act IV — About

Léa Moreau

Screenwriter & Artistic Auteur

Born in Lyon, raised between Paris and the countryside, Léa Moreau discovered cinema through literature. Her first scripts were written in the margins of novels — dialogue fragments hidden between the lines of Camus and Duras.

After studying at La Fémis and assisting several established screenwriters, she developed a signature style that privileges atmosphere over exposition. Her scripts are known for their rhythmic silences, their trust in the audience, and their refusal to explain what can be felt.

Her work has been selected at Cannes, Berlin, and Venice, and she has collaborated with directors across Europe and beyond. She believes that the best stories are the ones that trust the spaces between words.

What I Write

01

Original Screenplays

Feature-length original scripts built from concept to final draft. Character-driven stories that trust the audience's intelligence.

02

Adaptations

Translating literature, memoir, and true stories into cinematic language while preserving the soul of the source material.

03

Script Doctoring

Refining existing screenplays — strengthening structure, deepening character, and finding the story that was always there.

04

Series Development

Long-form narrative architecture for limited series and episodic formats. Building worlds that sustain across seasons.

Short Film Scripts

Economy of storytelling in its purest form. Every word earns its place on the page.

One-on-one and group sessions for emerging writers. Finding your voice, not someone else's.
