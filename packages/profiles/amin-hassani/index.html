04 ABOUT

I grew up in a house where my father repaired radios. The smell of solder and the hum of vacuum tubes are my earliest memories. By twelve, I was building my own amplifiers. By twenty, I was recording the sounds of Tehran's bazaar at dawn — the call to prayer mixing with the clatter of tea glasses and the distant rumble of the metro.

After studying electroacoustic composition at Berklee, I moved to Los Angeles and apprenticed at Skywalker Sound. Now I split my time between Tehran and LA, recording the sonic signatures of places most people never stop to listen to. My mixing stage is where those field recordings meet cinematic storytelling.
