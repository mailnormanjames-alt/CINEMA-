I. PHILOSOPHY

"Music doesn't accompany cinema — it breathes inside it. I compose for the space between frames, the heartbeat a director can't film, the breath an audience doesn't know they're holding."
01

Emotional Architecture

Every orchestration is a structure. I build sound like a cathedral — from the foundation of bass frequencies to the soaring heights of violins in resonance.

02

CinematicFrame

My composition system syncs musical phrasing to visual frames. Each cue is measured not in bars, but in cuts, dissolves, and the rhythm of the edit.

03

Grand Dramatic

From whispered piano to thundering brass — I compose in extremes. The concert hall is my canvas, the orchestra my palette of infinite color.

IV. ABOUT

Rafael Santos

Composer / Artistic Auteur

Rafael Santos grew up in the echo of concert halls, raised on the scores of Williams, Newman, and Morricone. He discovered that the space between notes carries more weight than the notes themselves. After studying composition at Juilliard, he scored his first independent feature at 24 — a low-budget drama that premiered at Sundance and changed the course of his career.

His approach — CinematicFrame — treats every visual cut as a musical rest, every fade as a diminuendo. He composes not to fill silence, but to give it shape. His work spans feature films, documentaries, and concert hall arrangements performed by philharmonic orchestras worldwide.
