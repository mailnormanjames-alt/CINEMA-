01 Process

Every set is a reality waiting to be inhabited.

Every material has a memory. Every wall tells a story. My work begins with the bones of a space — its structure, its light, its silence — and I build outward from there. Architecture is not backdrop; it is character.

I

Blueprint

Reading the script as an architect reads a site survey. Understanding load-bearing walls of narrative.

II

Foundation

Establishing spatial logic. Where does light fall? Where does silence settle? Building the emotional skeleton.

III

Construction

Physical build — from miniature maquettes to full-scale construction. Every surface considered, every texture intentional.

IV

Inhabitation

The moment actors enter and the space becomes alive. Architecture breathes through performance.

04 About
Self-Portrait as Site Survey

Yuki Tanaka works at the intersection of architecture and cinema, constructing spaces that exist somewhere between memory and blueprint.

Yuki Tanaka works at the intersection of architecture and cinema, constructing spaces that exist somewhere between memory and blueprint. She studied architecture at the University of Tokyo before transitioning to production design after realizing that film offered something architecture could not: the dimension of time. Every set she builds carries the weight of a building and the ephemerality of a dream.

Her work has been recognized at the Venice Architecture Biennale, the Tokyo International Film Festival, and the Academy Awards. She splits her time between Tokyo and Los Angeles, running TheWorkshop — her design studio and miniature fabrication lab.
